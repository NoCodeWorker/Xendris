v1.9 extends Phygn/Signphy into a new high-value domain:

```txt
business model validation.
```

The purpose is not to guarantee that a business will succeed.

The purpose is to convert business models into:

```txt
claims
hypotheses
observable metrics
customer tests
willingness-to-pay tests
channel experiments
unit economics checks
risk gates
kill criteria
```

---

## 1. Core thesis

```txt
A business model is a bundle of untested claims.
```

Phygn must decompose it into testable business hypotheses.

---

## 2. What Phygn can validate

Phygn can evaluate whether a business model has:

```txt
defined customer
real pain
urgent use case
willingness to pay
reachable channel
credible pricing
reasonable unit economics
clear differentiation
operational feasibility
risk/regulatory awareness
kill criteria
evidence trail
```

Phygn cannot guarantee:

```txt
market success
future revenue
customer adoption
funding
profitability
```

## 5. Product framing

User-facing wording:

```txt
Phygn does not validate your business by enthusiasm.
It turns the business into experiments that can survive contact with customers.
```

Signphy-facing wording:

