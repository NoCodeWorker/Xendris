## 2. Why Frontera C-Mayor Is Not Currently a Core Theory Candidate

Frontera C-Mayor is not currently a core theory candidate because its proposed layered structure has not produced a non-redundant theorem, operational prediction, or physical boundary beyond known frameworks.

The current formal structure:

```txt
D_CI(O) subset D_LC(O)
```

is useful as a disciplined classification. It blocks the invalid move from causal accessibility to measurability, coherence access, and recoverability. However, the individual layers are strongly covered by existing physics and information theory.

The current status is therefore:

```txt
bridge-only framework, not validated theory.
```

## 3. What AQFT Absorbed

The AQFT / Haag-Kastler comparison absorbed the causal and local-observable layers:

- `D_LC(O)` is strongly covered by causal region structure.
- `A_c(O,e)` is strongly covered by causal accessibility and microcausality.
- Much of `M(O,e)` is covered by local observable algebras and measurement-access structure.

The surviving layer after AQFT was not a new physical boundary. It was only the possible additional bridge ordering of information transfer, coherence access, and recoverability.

## 8. Conditions Required to Reopen CORE_THEORY_CANDIDATE Status

Reopening `CORE_THEORY_CANDIDATE` requires at least one of:

1. A formal theorem showing that `D_CI(O)` has nontrivial consequences not reducible to AQFT, causal structure, measurement theory, quantum information, decoherence, or recoverability.
2. A primary-source deep review showing that no existing framework already defines the same joint causal-information-coherence-recoverability boundary.
3. A reviewer-confirmed operational definition of `I_c`, `K`, and `R` that creates a falsifiable distinction rather than a restatement.
4. A worked model where `B_c(O)` classifies or excludes cases in a way not already captured by known frameworks.
5. External expert review classifying the structure as a formal theorem candidate rather than a bridge synthesis.

Until one of these occurs, `core_theory_candidate` remains false.

## 9. Conditions That Would Demote It Further to INTERPRETIVE_FRAME_ONLY

Frontera C-Mayor should be demoted to `INTERPRETIVE_FRAME_ONLY` if:

- primary-source review shows the joint `I/K/R` layer is already fully covered by known frameworks,
- `D_CI(O)` remains purely definitional without operational thresholds,
- `B_c(O)` cannot be distinguished from standard observability, decoherence, channel, or recovery boundaries,
- expert reviewers classify the framework as vocabulary rather than formal structure,
- no theorem, model, or operational decision procedure survives redundancy review.
