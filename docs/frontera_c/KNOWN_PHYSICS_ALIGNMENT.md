## 2. Core Hypothesis Under Review

Frontera C-Mayor asks whether `c` can be modeled not only as a maximum propagation speed, but also as a causal-informational membrane separating domains by their ability to exchange coherent, causal, measurable information with an observer.

## 3. What Known Physics Already Says

Known physics already establishes:

- `c` is invariant in special relativity.
- `c` defines the causal structure of Minkowski spacetime.
- Light cones separate timelike, lightlike, and spacelike intervals.
- No causal signal propagates outside the light cone in relativistic theories.
- General relativity admits horizons that bound causal communication.
- Observers can access only events in their causal past.
- Measurement is physically constrained by signal propagation, interaction, noise, and available channels.
- Quantum theory and decoherence describe limits on coherent phase information and measurement outcomes.

These are not Frontera C-Mayor discoveries.

## 4. Alignment Requirements

Frontera C-Mayor must not contradict:

- Lorentz invariance,
- relativistic causal ordering,
- no-superluminal-signalling constraints,
- standard horizon causal structure,
- existing quantum measurement constraints,
- established decoherence mechanisms.

Any proposed extension must be compatible with these unless it explicitly claims a contradiction, in which case the claim is high-risk and requires extraordinary support.

## 5. What Frontera C-Mayor Might Add

The possible addition is not that `c` limits speed. That is already known.

The possible addition is a unifying operational model:

```txt
c as causal-informational membrane
```

This would try to organize:

- causal accessibility,
- information transfer,
- observer-relative measurability,
- coherence and recoverability,
- inaccessible domains,
- indirect traces of inaccessible domains,
- boundaries between physical availability and physical inaccessibility.

## 6. Non-Novel Claims

The following claims are not novel:

- `c` is the speed limit of causal propagation.
- Spacelike-separated events cannot exchange ordinary causal signals.
- Light cones define causal accessibility.
- Horizons limit what observers can observe.
- Decoherence limits coherent measurement access.
- Information can be lost or inaccessible to a local observer.

If Frontera C-Mayor only restates these, it is non-novel.
