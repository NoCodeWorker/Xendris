## 2. Candidate Novelty Statement

A possible novelty claim is:

```txt
The invariant `c` can be used as the organizing boundary of a causal-informational membrane: a structure that classifies physical domains by possible coherent, causal, measurable information exchange with an observer.
```

## 3. What Would Be Non-Novel

Frontera C-Mayor is non-novel if it only says:

- `c` is a speed limit.
- Light cones define causal order.
- Horizons limit observability.
- Observers only measure what can physically interact with them.
- Decoherence makes phase information inaccessible.
- Information transfer requires physical channels.

Those are already part of existing physics or standard interpretation.

## 4. What Might Be Novel

Frontera C-Mayor might become novel if it provides one or more of:

1. A formal membrane variable or operator that unifies causal accessibility, coherent information transfer, and observer-measurement access.
2. A classification of physical domains that yields consequences not reducible to ordinary light-cone membership.
3. A falsifiable distinction between causal access and causal-informational recoverability.
4. A bridge between horizons, decoherence, and observer-relative information access that makes a new testable prediction.
5. A negative theorem showing where apparent membrane effects collapse to known physics.

## 5. Claim Classes

### Definition-Level Claim

### Structural Claim

Given a defined observer and causal domain, light-cone structure bounds possible direct information exchange.

Status: likely known physics unless the formalism adds something differentiated.

### Bridge Claim

```txt
Coherence or recoverability may be organized by causal accessibility constraints.
```

Status: bridge hypothesis, requires formalization.

### Novel Physics Claim

```txt
The membrane formalism predicts an observable, theorem, or constraint not already implied by relativity, quantum theory, or standard information theory.
```

Status: not currently established.

## 7. Auxiliary Boundary

Auxiliary results can suggest analogies but cannot create novelty by themselves.

Examples that do not establish novelty:

- visibility degradation,
- contrast loss,
- thermal or optical signal corruption,
- camera or sensor limits,
- PredictiveGain on an auxiliary benchmark,
- `heating_power_W` axis expansion.
