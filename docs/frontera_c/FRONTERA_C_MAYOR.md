# Frontera C-Mayor

## 1. Strict Definition

Frontera C-Mayor is the working hypothesis that `c` is not only the invariant maximum propagation speed in relativistic physics, but may also be treated as a causal-informational membrane: a structural boundary separating domains by whether they can exchange coherent, causal, measurable information with an observer.

## 2. Non-Definition: What It Is Not

Frontera C-Mayor is not a visibility, contrast, thermal, camera, sensor, LiDAR, image-processing, or `heating_power_W` benchmark. Those topics are auxiliary unless a formal bridge connects them back to causal accessibility, information transfer, coherence, observability, and `c`.

## 3. Central Hypothesis

The central question is whether `c` functions only as a velocity limit, or also organizes physical accessibility by defining which events, states, or domains can become coherently observable to a given observer.

## 4. Why `c` Is Central

`c` determines causal ordering in relativistic spacetime. It separates timelike, lightlike, and spacelike relations and therefore constrains what can exchange signals, influence, and measurable information.

## 5. Relation to Relativity

The project must remain aligned with known relativity. Any novelty claim must distinguish itself from standard light-cone causality, Lorentz invariance, horizon physics, and ordinary signal propagation limits.

## 6. Relation to Light Cones

Light cones are the immediate geometric expression of the membrane idea. They define the boundary between causally accessible and causally inaccessible events for an observer.

## 7. Relation to Horizons

Event horizons, cosmological horizons, and causal diamonds are candidate physical cases where the causal-informational membrane becomes operationally visible as a boundary of observability and recoverability.

## 8. Relation to Information Theory

The bridge layer concerns whether causal accessibility implies limits on information transfer, recoverability, compression, entropy accounting, or observer-relative knowledge.

## 9. Relation to Decoherence

Decoherence may be relevant only as a bridge: it concerns loss of coherent access to phase information. It does not by itself validate Frontera C-Mayor unless tied explicitly to `c` and causal structure.

## 10. Observer and Measurement

The observer is not an arbitrary subjective element. The relevant question is physical accessibility: what can be measured, inferred, or coherently reconstructed given causal constraints.

## 11. What Is Already Known

Known physics already contains invariant `c`, light cones, relativistic causality, horizons, decoherence, and observer-dependent access limitations. The project must not rebrand these as novelty without differentiation.

## 12. What Would Be New

A new contribution would require a precise formulation showing that `c` can be modeled as a causal-informational membrane with falsifiable consequences not reducible to standard causality, ordinary decoherence, or generic information loss.

## 13. Candidate Formal Variables

Preliminary variables may include:

- `c`
- causal accessibility
- information transfer
- coherence
- recoverability
- observer relation
- horizon boundary
- signal/information exchange

No final equation is accepted yet.

## 14. Falsifiability

Falsifiability requires a claim that can fail against known physics, mathematical consistency, or empirical observation. Auxiliary benchmarks do not satisfy this requirement unless a formal bridge is approved.

## 15. Validation Ladder

1. Stable definition.
2. Known-physics alignment.
3. Novelty separation.
4. Candidate formal model.
5. Falsifiable prediction or theorem.
6. Bridge validation, if needed.
7. Empirical or mathematical pressure.
8. Validation decision.

## 16. Current Status

Frontera C-Mayor is `NOT_VALIDATED`. The thermal-optical and heating-power work is `AUXILIARY_ONLY` and does not advance validation status.

## 17. Open Problems

- Define the membrane formally without duplicating light-cone causality.
- Separate novelty from existing relativity and horizon physics.
- State falsifiable consequences.
- Decide which bridge topics are necessary.
- Keep auxiliary studies from replacing the core hypothesis.

Frontera C-Mayor remains a hypothesis. The current priority is formal definition, known-physics alignment, novelty separation, and falsifiability.
